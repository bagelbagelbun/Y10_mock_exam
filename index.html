How to Actually Revise

  • Little But Often: Instead of spending a long time revising in the final few days before the exam, start your revision as soon as possible and revise in short sessions more regularly
  • Active Revision: Reading over notes and highlighting is not as useful as testing yourself. Use the flashcards or exam questions on this site to practice the skill of RETRIEVAL - remembering things under pressure.
  • Get to Know Your Texts: look at your set texts often, and make notes on them as you do so. Choose whether to focus on MEDIA LANGUAGE or REPRESENTATION, and then note down everything you notice.
  • Be Creative: Write songs and poems about the topics, make up acronyms (like RISE), record video explainers, use images and colours in your notes. All these sensory techniques can make it much easier to remember complex concepts!
